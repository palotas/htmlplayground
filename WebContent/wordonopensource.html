A word on open source

While Open Source software is free and cost is an ever important factor for businesses, there are many more great reasons to use open source in your company. Open source enables you to innovate within your organization at the speed that you need to. If you are using 3rd party vendor tools you are at the mercy of the tool vendor and you rely completely on an external development or innovation cycle. So it is kind of like allowing a 3rd party to decide how quickly you can grow and expand your business.

Open source certainly requires a different mindset in order to be successful. While open source gives you a lot of flexibility it also puts a lot of responsibility into your hands - which we think is a good thing. Open Source empowers you to use the software in the way you need to (and not how a 3rd party vendor prescribes it to you) You are in the driver seat and can decide how fast (or slow) you go.

In today's world good engineers are very scarce. Hiring (or the inability to hire) is one of the main factors why companies cannot stay competitive.

  • access to much larger resource pool
  • greatly reduced ramp up times for new hires
